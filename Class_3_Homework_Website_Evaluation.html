Amazon

Amazon.com uses a dark gray as the main color with an orange secondary color. The color matches even though it is so simple.

The primary users of the site are adult ages 18-55 and the chosen design works well because of its simplicity and welcoming feel.

User tasks are to purchase items or price match and it is very easy to do. The user may also sign up for one-click purchasing inorder to make the process faster.
This benfits the company more than the user, because they are more likely to comppulsively purchase items.

This site provides an excellent and user friendly experience.

Facebook

Facebook.com is another website that I frequently visit. The primary color is blue and there is a ton of white.
I feel that this is welcoming and matches the site.

The primary users for this site is pretty general and the design caters the audience due to its simplicity.

Users tasks and goals are to stalk family and friends. This task is very easy to do as it is easy to search for
who you want or just scroll through the news feed.

User experience is excellent, and makes the user constantly frequent the website.

Southwest Airlines

Southwest Airlines is another site I frequent. The site main color is blue with a soft gray over lying the website.
There is an image in the bakc ground that stays in position as the user scrolls giving the page depth.

The gray fades from light gray into white gradually. This site is very smooth.

Primary users on this site are men and women ages 24-44 and the layout is very well done.
The goal is to search for flights and book. The site does cater to its user by making it simple and has such an easy layout.

I believe the site does provide a great user experience. When booking flight they have the low fare calender
which shows how much the flights cost on any day of the month. Google Flights now uses the same funtionality for its users.
